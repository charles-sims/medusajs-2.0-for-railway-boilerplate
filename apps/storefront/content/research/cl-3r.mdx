<MolecularStructure src="cl-3r" />

CL-3R is a triple receptor agonist compound targeting the glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide receptor (GIPR), glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor (GLP-1R), and glucagon receptor (GcgR) simultaneously. CL-3R is a 39-amino acid acylated peptide engineered to engage all three related class B G protein-coupled receptors of the glucagon peptide superfamily in non-clinical experimental systems. The simultaneous engagement of GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon signaling axes — sometimes described as "triagonism" — represents the most mechanistically expansive approach to incretin-related metabolic research currently under preclinical investigation.

The addition of glucagon receptor agonism to the dual incretin profile distinguishes CL-3R from single- and dual-incretin research tools. In non-clinical models, glucagon receptor activation is associated with hepatic glucose production, thermogenesis through brown adipose tissue engagement, and lipolytic signaling — providing an additional dimension of metabolic pathway investigation not accessible through GLP-1 or dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor tools alone. CL-3R is used in preclinical research to probe the intersection of these three receptor systems in experimental metabolic models.

## Mechanism of Action

CL-3R engages GIPR, GLP-1R, and GcgR in experimental systems, each of which signals through Gs protein coupling to adenylyl cyclase, elevating intracellular cAMP and activating PKA-dependent downstream cascades in non-clinical cell preparations.<Cite id={1} /> The concurrent activation of all three receptor types in the same experimental system produces a composite pharmacological profile distinguishable from that achieved by individual or dual receptor engagement.

In non-clinical hepatic models, GcgR activation has been studied for its role in gluconeogenic gene expression, glycogenolysis, and lipid oxidation signaling in experimental liver tissue preparations.<Cite id={2} /> In preclinical brown adipose tissue models, glucagon receptor stimulation has been associated with uncoupling protein-1 (UCP1) upregulation and thermogenic program activation in experimental settings. The dual incretin components contribute beta-cell protective and appetite-regulatory signaling in animal models, while glucagon receptor engagement adds a thermogenic and lipolytic dimension to the overall preclinical research profile.<Cite id={3} /> This multi-receptor engagement enables investigation of inter-pathway interactions and potential mechanistic synergies in experimental metabolic systems.<Cite id={4} />

<ResearchApplications>
<Application title="Multi-receptor Pathway Research">
Preclinical pharmacology research has employed triple agonist compounds to investigate the mechanistic contributions of simultaneous GIPR, GLP-1R, and GcgR agonism in experimental cell and animal systems. In non-clinical receptor pharmacology studies, the relative agonist activity at each receptor has been characterized through cAMP accumulation assays, receptor internalization measurements, and downstream signaling readouts in transfected cell models. These studies inform understanding of poly-agonist receptor biology in experimental systems.<Cite id={1} />
</Application>

<Application title="Energy Expenditure Models">
Non-clinical energy expenditure research has utilized triple agonist tools to investigate the combined contributions of incretin signaling and glucagon receptor activation to thermogenesis and metabolic rate in preclinical animal models. In experimental indirect calorimetry studies, triple agonist compounds have been associated with increased oxygen consumption and altered respiratory exchange ratios compared to dual incretin controls in non-clinical settings, suggesting enhanced thermogenic pathway engagement mediated in part through GcgR agonism in brown adipose tissue.<Cite id={3} />
</Application>

<Application title="Metabolic Research">
Comprehensive preclinical metabolic research has employed CL-3R to simultaneously probe glucose homeostasis, lipid metabolism, and energy balance pathways in experimental models. In non-clinical obese rodent models, triple agonist compounds have been studied across hepatic, adipose, pancreatic, and CNS tissue compartments in parallel, providing a systems-level view of multi-receptor metabolic signaling in experimental settings that complements the more focused data obtained from single- and dual-receptor research tools.<Cite id={5} />
</Application>

<Application title="Glucagon Signaling Studies">
The inclusion of GcgR agonism in CL-3R makes it a distinct tool for preclinical glucagon signaling research in the context of concurrent incretin pathway activation. Non-clinical studies have used triple agonist compounds to investigate hepatic glucose metabolism, gluconeogenic enzyme regulation, and the modulation of glucagon counter-regulatory responses in experimental models of metabolic disease. This research context is not accessible through GLP-1R or dual incretin tools and represents a unique application of triple agonist compounds in experimental systems.<Cite id={2} />
</Application>

## Handling and Storage Requirements

**Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Researchers should wear nitrile gloves, laboratory coat, and safety goggles when handling CL-3R in any form. Work in a well-ventilated area or under a fume hood when reconstituting lyophilized material.

**Storage Conditions:** Store lyophilized CL-3R at -20 degrees C in a desiccated environment, protected from light. Reconstituted solutions should be stored at 2-8 degrees C and used within 48 hours, or aliquoted and stored at -20 degrees C to minimize freeze-thaw degradation. As with all acylated peptides, exposure to repeated freeze-thaw cycles should be minimized to preserve structural integrity.

**Spill Response:** In the event of a spill, contain the area with absorbent material, collect waste into an appropriate chemical waste container, and decontaminate the surface with 70% ethanol followed by water rinse. Dispose of all contaminated materials in accordance with institutional chemical waste protocols.

**Documentation Requirements:** Maintain detailed records of lot numbers, reconstitution dates, storage conditions, and experimental usage in compliance with institutional research protocols. All handling must conform to your institution's chemical hygiene plan and applicable regulatory requirements.
