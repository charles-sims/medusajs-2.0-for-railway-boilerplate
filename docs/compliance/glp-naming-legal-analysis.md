# GLP Naming Convention & Legal Analysis

**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Purpose:** Analyze competitor GLP compound naming strategies and recommend CaliLean's approach to minimize legal exposure while maintaining market clarity.

---

## The Pattern: Company Acronym + Number + Compound Initial

Every competitor uses the same strategy: **avoid the generic drug name on the label** and replace it with a branded code derived from their company name. The generic name (Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Retatrutide) appears only in SEO tags, product descriptions, or search metadata — never on the physical label or primary product name.

### Competitor Naming Matrix

| Competitor | Semaglutide | Tirzepatide | Retatrutide | Pattern |
|-----------|------------|------------|------------|---------|
| **Ion Peptide** | ION-1S | ION-2T | ION-3R | `[BRAND]-[GLP#][Initial]` |
| **Purgo Labs** | (not listed) | GLP-2 T | GLP-3 R | `GLP-[#] [Initial]` |
| **Flawless Compounds** | GLP1-S | GLP2-T | GLP3-R | `GLP[#]-[Initial]` |
| **Mile High Compounds** | GLP-1 SM / MHC-1-SM | GLP-2 TRZ / MHC-2-TRZ | GLP-3 RT / MHC-3-RT | `GLP-[#] [Abbrev]` or `MHC-[#]-[Abbrev]` |
| **Crush Research** | Agonist S | Double Agonist T | Triple Agonist R | `[Mechanism] Agonist [Initial]` |
| **CaliLean (current)** | GLP1-S | GLP2-T | GLP3-R | `GLP[#]-[Initial]` (matches Flawless) |

### Naming Strategy Tiers

**Tier 1 — Most Abstracted (Safest)**
- **Ion Peptide**: `ION-1S`, `ION-2T`, `ION-3R` — Uses company acronym. Zero reference to GLP or agonist mechanism. Most insulated from trademark/regulatory claims.
- **Crush Research**: `Agonist S`, `Double Agonist T`, `Triple Agonist R` — Describes mechanism of action, not the compound. Equally abstracted.

**Tier 2 — Mechanism Reference (Moderate)**
- **Purgo Labs**: `GLP-2 T`, `GLP-3 R` — References the GLP receptor class (which is a scientific term, not a trademark) plus an initial.
- **Flawless Compounds**: `GLP1-S`, `GLP2-T`, `GLP3-R` — Same approach, slightly different formatting.
- **CaliLean (current)**: `GLP1-S`, `GLP2-T`, `GLP3-R` — Identical to Flawless.

**Tier 3 — Dual Naming (Riskier)**
- **Mile High Compounds**: Uses BOTH `GLP-3 RT` AND "Retatrutide" in the same product title ("GLP-3 RT-30 retatrutide"). The generic drug name appears in the H1 heading, URL slug, tags, and product description. This is the most exposed approach.

## Legal Risk Analysis

### Why Competitors Avoid Generic Drug Names

1. **FDA Regulatory Risk**: Semaglutide and Tirzepatide are FDA-approved drugs (Ozempic/Wegovy and Mounjaro/Zepbound). Labeling a research compound with the same name as an FDA-approved drug invites scrutiny — it suggests the product IS the drug, which triggers misbranding and unapproved drug distribution concerns under 21 USC 331.

2. **Trademark Exposure**: While generic chemical names (semaglutide, tirzepatide) are not trademarkable, Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk actively monitor the market. Using their drug names in product titles, labels, or advertising can attract cease-and-desist letters even if technically defensible.

3. **Payment Processor & Banking**: Multiple competitors (Flawless, Mile High, Ion Peptide) require account creation before showing products. This is explicitly to satisfy banking/payment processor compliance requirements. Processors flag accounts selling compounds with FDA-approved drug names.

4. **Retatrutide**: Still in clinical trials (not yet FDA-approved), so less trademark/regulatory risk, but Eli Lilly owns the INN (international nonproprietary name). Using it directly still creates exposure.

### CaliLean's Current Approach

**Current labels say:** "RESEARCH GRADE RETATRUTIDE (30MG)" — the generic drug name is printed directly on the vial label and box. This is the **highest-risk approach** among all competitors analyzed.

**Current product codes:** GLP1-S, GLP2-T, GLP3-R — These are fine and match industry standard (Tier 2).

### Risk Assessment

## Recommendations for CaliLean

### 1. Adopt Company-Prefix Naming (Tier 1)

Use **CL-1S**, **CL-2T**, **CL-3R** as the primary product names:

| Current | Recommended | Full Display Name |
|---------|------------|-------------------|
| GLP1-S | **CL-1S** | CL-1S (GLP-1 Receptor Agonist) |
| GLP2-T | **CL-2T** | CL-2T (Dual GLP-1/GIP Agonist) |
| GLP3-R | **CL-3R** | CL-3R (Triple GLP-1/GIP/Glucagon Agonist) |

**Why CL-prefix:** "CL" = CaliLean. Follows the exact pattern of Ion Peptide (ION-) and Mile High (MHC-). Short, brandable, memorable.

**Alternative:** Keep GLP1-S / GLP2-T / GLP3-R (matches Flawless/Purgo, Tier 2). This is acceptable but less differentiated and doesn't build brand equity the way ION-1S does for Ion Peptide.

### 2. Remove Generic Drug Names from Physical Labels

**Vial label should read:**
```
CaliLean
RESEARCH GRADE
CL-3R (30MG)
```
NOT: "RESEARCH GRADE RETATRUTIDE (30MG)"

**Box side text should read:**
```
30mg Total
CL-3R Research Grade Compound
GLP-1/GIP/Glucagon Triple Receptor Agonist
Research Use Only - Not for human consumption
Keep refrigerated
```
NOT: "Retatrutide Research Grade Solution"

### 3. Use Generic Names Only in Web Content (Never on Labels)

- **Product page title**: "CL-3R" or "GLP3-R"
- **Product description**: May reference "triple-receptor agonist compound (retatrutide analog)" in body text for SEO
- **SEO meta/tags**: Can include semaglutide, tirzepatide, retatrutide for search discoverability (every competitor does this)
- **Physical label/box**: NEVER include the generic drug name

### 4. Required Disclaimer Language

Based on competitor analysis, every label and product page should include:

**Short form (vial/box):**
> Research Use Only. Not for human or animal consumption.

**Long form (product page/insert):**
> This product is sold as a research compound for in vitro laboratory use only. It is not intended for human or animal consumption, diagnostic, therapeutic, or clinical use. This product has not been evaluated or approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). By purchasing, you confirm you are a qualified researcher conducting lawful scientific research.

## Summary

**CaliLean is currently the ONLY competitor analyzed that prints the generic drug name directly on physical labels.** This should be corrected in the next print run. The recommended approach:

1. **Labels**: `CL-1S` / `CL-2T` / `CL-3R` with mechanism description, no generic drug names
2. **Website**: Generic names in descriptions and SEO only, not in product titles
3. **SKU codes**: Keep existing `CL-GL1-0010` etc. for internal use
4. **Disclaimers**: Add full research-only language to all labels, boxes, and product pages
