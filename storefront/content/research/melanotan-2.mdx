<Overview>
<MolecularStructure src="melanotan-2" />

Melanotan 2 (MT-II) is a cyclic heptapeptide and synthetic analog of alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH), a tridecapeptide derived from post-translational processing of pro-opiomelanocortin (POMC). The structure of Melanotan 2 — Ac-Nle-cyclo[Asp-His-D-Phe-Arg-Trp-Lys]-NH₂ — incorporates a lactam bridge between the aspartic acid and lysine residues, conferring enhanced metabolic stability relative to the linear α-MSH parent molecule. It was first synthesized at the University of Arizona in the 1980s as part of a program investigating melanocortin biology in non-clinical models.

Unlike linear α-MSH, Melanotan 2 is a non-selective melanocortin receptor agonist, exhibiting affinity for MC1R, MC3R, MC4R, and MC5R receptor subtypes in preclinical binding studies. This promiscuous receptor engagement profile distinguishes it from more selective melanocortin tool compounds and has made it the subject of extensive non-clinical pharmacological characterization across multiple receptor-defined research domains. The cyclic backbone enhances resistance to proteolytic degradation in experimental in-vitro systems compared to its linear analogs.

## Mechanism of Action

The primary mechanism of Melanotan 2 in experimental systems involves agonist activation of melanocortin G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs), which signal primarily through Gαs-mediated adenylyl cyclase stimulation to elevate intracellular cyclic AMP (cAMP) concentrations in non-clinical cell models.<Cite id={1} /> Receptor-specific downstream consequences differ across MC receptor subtypes and have been characterized separately in preclinical model systems.

At MC1R — the melanocortin receptor expressed on melanocytes — cAMP elevation activates protein kinase A (PKA), which in turn phosphorylates CREB and upregulates microphthalmia-associated transcription factor (MITF), the master regulator of melanogenesis gene programs in experimental systems.<Cite id={2} /> This cascade drives transcriptional upregulation of tyrosinase, TRP-1, and TRP-2 — the core enzymatic machinery of eumelanin synthesis — in non-clinical melanocyte culture and pigmentation models. The resulting shift toward eumelanin production over phaeomelanin in experimental systems is the basis of Melanotan 2's characterization in photoprotection research models.

MC4R activation by Melanotan 2 in non-clinical central nervous system models has been associated with distinct signaling outcomes relevant to hypothalamic regulatory pathways in experimental neuroscience research.<Cite id={3} /> These activities at MC4R are studied separately from the pigmentation-related MC1R pathway in preclinical model systems, reflecting the divergent physiological roles of individual MC receptor subtypes in non-clinical biology.

<ResearchApplications>
<Application title="Melanogenesis Research">
In preclinical melanogenesis studies, Melanotan 2 has been used as a tool compound to characterize the molecular cascade linking MC1R activation to eumelanin synthesis in non-clinical melanocyte models. Experimental cell culture systems using B16 melanoma cells and primary melanocyte cultures report dose-dependent increases in melanin content, tyrosinase activity, and MITF expression following MC1R agonist treatment under controlled experimental conditions.<Cite id={2} /> These observations are confined to non-clinical in-vitro and animal experimental systems.
</Application>

<Application title="Melanocortin Receptor Studies">
Non-clinical pharmacological research has employed Melanotan 2 as a reference agonist in melanocortin receptor binding and functional assays. In preclinical radioligand displacement studies and cAMP accumulation assays, Melanotan 2 has been characterized across all four peripheral and central MC receptor subtypes to establish binding affinity constants (Ki), agonist potency (EC50), and efficacy profiles for comparative pharmacology research in experimental systems.<Cite id={1} /> These in-vitro characterizations inform the development of more selective melanocortin tool compounds in non-clinical research programs.
</Application>

<Application title="Photoprotection Models">
Preclinical photoprotection research has investigated MC1R agonism as a mechanism influencing UV-induced DNA damage responses in non-clinical melanocyte and skin explant models. In experimental systems, MC1R activation by α-MSH analogs has been associated with upregulation of nucleotide excision repair (NER) gene programs and increased eumelanin deposition, both of which influence UV photon absorption characteristics in experimental skin biology models.<Cite id={4} /> All findings are from non-clinical experimental systems only.
</Application>

<Application title="MC Receptor Selectivity Research">
The non-selective receptor profile of Melanotan 2 has made it a valuable reference compound in non-clinical studies investigating MC receptor subtype contributions to specific pharmacological outcomes. Preclinical selectivity studies compare the activity profiles of Melanotan 2 against more selective MC receptor agonists and antagonists to deconvolute receptor-specific contributions in experimental model systems.<Cite id={5} /> These structure-activity relationship (SAR) investigations are conducted in non-clinical in-vitro binding assays and transgenic animal models.
</Application>
</ResearchApplications>

## Handling and Storage Requirements

**Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Researchers should wear nitrile gloves, laboratory coat, and safety goggles when handling Melanotan 2 in any form. Work in a well-ventilated area or under a fume hood when reconstituting lyophilized material to avoid inhalation of particulate matter.

**Storage Conditions:** Store lyophilized Melanotan 2 at -20 degrees C in a desiccated, light-protected environment. Reconstituted solutions should be stored at 2-8 degrees C and used within 48 hours, or aliquoted and stored at -20 degrees C to minimize freeze-thaw degradation. The cyclic peptide backbone confers improved aqueous stability relative to linear analogs; however, avoid prolonged storage of reconstituted material at room temperature.

**Spill Response:** Contain spills with absorbent material, collect waste into an appropriate chemical waste container, and decontaminate the surface with 70% ethanol followed by water rinse. Dispose of all contaminated materials in accordance with institutional chemical waste protocols.

**Documentation Requirements:** Maintain detailed records of lot numbers, reconstitution dates, storage conditions, and experimental usage in compliance with institutional research protocols. All handling must conform to your institution's chemical hygiene plan and applicable regulatory requirements.
