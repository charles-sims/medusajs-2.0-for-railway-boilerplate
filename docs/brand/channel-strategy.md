## 0. The frame

We have one strategic constraint and one tactical constraint.

**Strategic:** RUO posture. We sell research compounds to qualified researchers. Most ad platforms classify peptides as restricted or prohibited (Meta, TikTok, Google all flag GLP-1, BPC-157, TB-500-class compounds). That is fine. **We were never going to win on paid acquisition.** We win on transparency, geography, and the founding-customer flywheel.

**Tactical:** South Bay is the proof market, not the only market. Every channel choice in the first 90 days has to make the local-first signal louder, not dilute it.

**Channel priorities (in rank order):**

1. **Owned: storefront + email + lab book.** The most defensible. The lab book is a channel.
2. **IRL: South Bay venues + run/tri/surf clubs + the Strand pop-up.** The activation that earned media will write about.
3. **Earned: press + organic creator seeding.** Free if the story is right. The story is right.
4. **Organic social: IG + TikTok.** Founder-led + UGC. Slow build. The depth is the strategy, not the volume.
5. **Paid: narrowly geo-fenced, narrowly worded.** Last on the list. ≤$2k/wk in window 1. ≤$8k/wk by day 90.

Anything that flips this rank order — including a paid agency that wants to spend $40k/mo in week one — is wrong for this brand at this stage. Bring it to CMO.

## 2. Organic social — IG + TikTok

### 2.1 The principle

We are not trying to be loud. We are trying to be **the account a researcher saves**. That means depth, restraint, and a single visual language that doesn't drift between posts.

**Three formats. Two voices. One cadence per channel.**

### 2.2 Format mix

| Format | Use | IG | TikTok | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — COA / batch carousel (still)** | The signature post. Mass-spec trace, batch number, "what's in the vial" overlaid in Söhne Mono. | ✅ feed | ✅ slideshow | 2× / week |
| **B — Strand still-life (photo or 5–8s clip)** | Vial on concrete, on tile, on a clipboard. Pre-dawn. No people in frame on this format. | ✅ feed + story | ✅ short clip | 2× / week |
| **C — Founder / advisor talking-head (60–90s)** | CMO, CEO, or medical advisor explains one peptide's primary literature. No claims, just citation reads. | ✅ Reels | ✅ native | 1× / week |
| **D — UGC repost (founder customers)** | Lab book on coffee table, vial in someone's lab/home gym. Permission required, attribution every time. | ✅ Stories + occasional feed | ✅ stitch / repost | 1–3× / week (depends on flow) |
| **E — Education-hub teaser (link-out)** | 1–2 frames pulled from a long-form essay; CTA is "read the full citation." | ✅ feed + bio link | ✅ caption-link | 1× / week |

**What we don't do:**
- Trends. We do not chant "GRWM as a peptide researcher" over a Subway Surfers gameplay.
- Influencer dance overlays.
- Before/after photos. Ever.
- Emoji in feed captions. (Stories, sparingly.)
- Therapeutic claims, dosing in any form, medical advice. **A post that crosses this gets pulled and rewritten — same day.**

### 2.3 Voices

- **Founder voice (CEO + CMO).** First person plural. The brand explains itself. Tone is identity-brief §3.
- **Researcher voice (medical advisor / future copywriter).** Third-person, footnote-led. Cites primary literature. Used in Format C and Format E.

UGC is reposted in either voice (caption rewritten by us before reposting). We do not publish a customer's caption verbatim — they're the photo, we're the copy.

### 2.4 Cadence

| Channel | Posts / week (window 1) | Stories / week | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Instagram** | 5 (Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri/Sat) | 8–12 | Saturday post is Strand-anchored every week. Sunday is dark. |
| **TikTok** | 3 (Tue/Thu/Sat) | n/a | Mirror Format A and Format C from IG; do not crosspost B as static — TikTok eats stills. |
| **Threads / Bluesky** | Off in window 1. CMO posts personally, not as the brand. | — | Open in window 3 if community justifies it. |
| **YouTube Shorts** | Off in window 1. | — | Open in window 2 with Format C re-cut. |

Cadence ramps: window 2 → 6 IG/wk + 4 TikTok/wk. Window 3 → 7 IG/wk + 5 TikTok/wk + Shorts.

### 2.5 Content pipeline

- **Founder voice scripts:** CMO writes briefs. Copywriter (when hired) drafts. CMO approves.
- **Researcher voice scripts:** medical advisor writes the science, CMO/Copywriter edits to brand voice without touching the citations.
- **Production:** in-house phone capture for window 1 (founder talking-heads, Strand stills). One paid shoot day with a local photographer in week 2 to bank Format B variations for the rest of the quarter.
- **Approval gate:** every post crosses CMO desk before it ships in window 1. The copywriter takes daily approval over from CMO once trust is set (~day 30).

- **Email 1 (T+0 from signup):** *"Why we publish the label."* Sender: CEO. ~80 words. Links: positioning page + sample COA.
- **Email 2 (T+5 days):** *"What's a peptide? What's a COA?"* Sender: brand. ~120 words. Links: education hub.
- **Email 3 (T+12 days):** *"Founding-100 spots open [date]."* Sender: brand. ~50 words. CTA: pre-register for the founding window.

### 4.2 Founding-100 launch flow (F2) — message ladder

- **Email 1 (T+0, redemption confirmation):** transactional. Single line + receipt + COA link.
- **Email 2 (T+3 days):** *"Your lab book is in the mail."* Sender: CEO. Numbered slot called out in copy.
- **Email 3 (T+14 days):** *"What we're hearing in the first 14 days."* Sender: CEO. ~100 words. No metrics, no chest-thumping.
- **Email 4 (T+30 days):** *"Batch update + the second SKU wave."* Sender: brand. Includes new COAs and a quiet referral CTA.

### 4.3 Education series (F3) — message ladder

One email per essay published on the education hub. Format is consistent: opening sentence quotes a primary paper. Two paragraphs of brand voice. Citation list. Footer links: full essay + COA for the relevant peptide.

### 4.4 Voice & ops rules

- **From-name:** "CaliLean" for transactional, "Joe / CaliLean" (CEO first name) for founder-voice emails. Never "Marketing Team."
- **Subject lines:** lowercase, short (≤6 words). Examples: `read the label.`, `your lab book is in the mail.`, `batch 240526-A is live.`
- **No exclamation points. No emoji.** Including in the welcome flow.
- **No discount codes in window 1.** A single 10% friend-of-the-founder offer can ship in F2 email 4 if (and only if) day-30 KPIs are met.
- **Plain-text variant:** ship every email as both HTML and plain text. Plain-text reads research, plays well in dark mode.
- **ESP:** Klaviyo (CTO confirms wiring under [SKA-4](/SKA/issues/SKA-4)). Domain auth + DMARC strict before day 0.

## 5. Paid

### 5.1 The constraint

Peptides — and especially BPC-157, TB-500, and any GLP-1-class compound — sit in restricted or prohibited categories on Meta, TikTok, and Google. Add the RUO posture (research-use-only, not for human consumption) and the surface area for compliant paid copy is **small but non-zero**.

What we **cannot** do:

- Promote any specific peptide by name as a consumer product on Meta or TikTok.
- Make therapeutic, dosing, or "before/after" claims anywhere.
- Run pharmacy / Rx / supplement-style ads — wrong category, accelerated takedown.
- Buy a peptide-keyword campaign on Google Ads (will be denied or yanked within hours).

What we **can** do:

- Run **brand-only** awareness creative ("CaliLean — research-grade peptides, plainly labeled, South Bay") with no specific product imagery and no therapeutic claims. Meta and TikTok approve this in our category about 60–70% of the time on first review; resubmit with copy adjustments when denied.
- Geo-fence aggressively: South Bay zips + Westside + PV. National geos are a waste of money and a takedown risk.
- Buy **Google Ads on brand-name terms only** (`calilean`, `calilean peptides`). Defends the SERP from competitor squatting.
- Buy **podcast ads on transparency-aligned shows** (Huberman is unrealistic; longevity-podcast ad networks have inventory we can buy without a creator endorsement). Read by host but copy is ours: the "we publish the certificate of analysis" beat. Window 3.
- Run **OOH** — one PCH billboard in window 2 (already in the launch narrative). OOH is unregulated for our category.
- Run **direct mail** — a windowed campaign to South Bay zip codes with a card that mirrors the launch creative. Windows 2 and 3.

### 5.2 Budget envelope (window 1)

| Line | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Meta brand-awareness, geo-fenced (SB + Westside + PV) | $500/wk | Brand-only creative, Beat 1 cuts. Approve creative with the platform before scale. |
| TikTok brand-awareness (same geo) | $500/wk | Same constraints. |
| Google Ads — brand-name defense | $200/wk | `calilean`, exact + phrase. |
| Reserve for promoted post tests | $300/wk | Boost top-performing organic, single-creative tests. |
| **Window 1 weekly total** | **≤$1,500** | Ramp only if first-week creative approves and CTR ≥ baseline. |

### 5.3 Budget envelope (windows 2–3)

- Window 2 (days 30–60): up to $5k/wk across paid social + branded search + OOH amortized.
- Window 3 (days 60–90): up to $8k/wk; layer in podcast inventory + direct mail wave.
- **Hard ceiling for the 90-day window: $40k.** Anything above goes to the CEO for explicit sign-off.

### 5.4 What we measure on paid

- **Reach in target zips** (SB / Westside / PV). Not national.
- **Branded search lift** in week-over-week (proxy for offline + creator + IRL pulling people to search the brand).
- **Direct traffic** % of total — must rise as paid scales. If paid scales and direct doesn't follow, paid is buying clicks, not customers.
- **Disapproval rate.** Tracked by week. If it climbs past 30%, we are drifting into restricted copy and the agency / Growth hire pulls back.
- **CAC payback on paid-attributed orders.** Target ≤ 90 days. If paid CAC payback runs > 180 days, it is a brand investment, not a performance one — call it that on the budget line.
