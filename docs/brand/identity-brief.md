# CaliLean — Brand Identity Brief

**Owner:** CMO
**Status:** v1 (foundation document — designer + copy build against this)
**Last updated:** 2026-04-26

This brief is the source of truth for what CaliLean is, how it sounds, and what it looks like. Anything downstream (logo system, packaging, storefront copy, ads, email, packaging, OG cards, founder script) should be testable against the rules here. If something on the storefront fails one of these tests, it's wrong — flag it, don't ship it.

We are explicitly **not** Bluum. Bluum's pastel + rounded-grotesque (Switzer) direction reads as soft consumer-wellness. CaliLean reads as quietly elite — coastal, clinical, considered. Closer to *Aesop x Tracksmith x Function Health* than to *Athletic Greens x Recess*.

---

## 1. Positioning

### One-sentence

**CaliLean is the clinically formulated peptide line for the South Bay athlete who plans to still be running the Strand at sixty.**

### One-paragraph

CaliLean makes peptide protocols for adults who treat training, sleep, and bloodwork as a daily practice — not a phase. Every product is formulated against a single question: does this measurably help a serious recreational athlete recover faster, hold lean mass longer, and extend healthspan without compromising trust? We answer that with a published clinician of record, third-party assayed lots, plain-English protocols, and a refusal to chase trends. We are based in the South Bay because our customers are. The brand is built for people who will read the COA, ask about the half-life, and then go run six miles before work.

### What we are / are not

| We are | We are not |
| --- | --- |
| Clinically formulated, clinician-fronted | A gray-market research-peptide reseller |
| Coastal-minimal, considered, premium | Beach-bro, neon, "biohacker maximalist" |
| Plain-spoken expert | Influencer-voiced, hype-driven |
| Local-first (South Bay → CA → US) | Trying to be everywhere on day one |
| "Lean" = body comp + ops + ingredients | A bodybuilder cut-stack brand |

## 2. Audience

**Primary persona — "The Strand Runner."**
35–55. Household income $300k+. Lives in Manhattan Beach, Hermosa, Redondo, El Segundo, or Palos Verdes. Trains 5–6x/week (run + lift + Pilates/yoga + ocean). Listens to Huberman, Attia, and Rich Roll. Already takes AG1 or LMNT, has a CGM at least seasonally, has had a Function Health or InsideTracker panel. Reads labels. Will pay a premium for trust. Skeptical of anything that markets like a supplement. Will not tolerate being talked down to.

**Secondary — "The Returning Athlete."**
Former college/club athlete in their 40s who is rebuilding capacity after kids/career. Recovery is the gateway product (BPC-157, TB-500). Buys in for performance, stays for longevity.

**Tertiary — "The Longevity Curious."**
Tech/finance professional, less athletic but data-driven. Enters via NAD+ / GHK-Cu / sleep stack. Drives AOV.

## 3. Voice

### Voice principles

1. **Plain expert.** A clinician explaining a protocol to a smart adult, not a wellness influencer or a lab tech.
2. **Confidence without hype.** Specific numbers beat adjectives. "5mg/ml, subcutaneous, 5-day cycle" beats "powerful recovery formula."
3. **Restraint.** Short sentences. White space. The product is the sentence. Don't oversell — the audience punishes overselling.
4. **Local cadence.** Reference the Strand, the South Bay, the 6am ocean run when it's earned. Never as costume.
5. **Earned humor, dry.** A small wink — never a punchline. The reader should feel respected, not entertained.

### Five do / don't pairs

| Do | Don't |
| --- | --- |
| "BPC-157, 250mcg, twice daily, four weeks." | "Unlock your body's healing potential." |
| "Sourced from a US cGMP facility. COA on every lot." | "Premium quality you can trust." |
| "We don't sell stacks we wouldn't run ourselves." | "Trusted by hundreds of athletes worldwide." |
| "Built in the South Bay. Tested on the Strand." | "California vibes meet cutting-edge science." |
| "Talk to a clinician before you order." | "DM us for a custom protocol." |

### Three voice exemplars

**A. Homepage hero (45 words)**

> Peptides, made plain.
> Recovery, leanness, longevity — formulated against the standard a clinician would actually run on themselves. Third-party assayed. Reviewed by our medical director. Built in the South Bay for adults who plan to still be running at sixty.

**B. PDP — BPC-157 (90 words)**

> **BPC-157 — 5mg vial**
> A 15-amino-acid peptide derived from a protein found in human gastric juice. Used for soft-tissue and gut recovery.
>
> **Typical protocol.** 250mcg subcutaneous, twice daily, in 4-week cycles. Pair with adequate sleep and protein — peptides amplify recovery, they don't replace it.
> **What's in the box.** One 5mg lyophilized vial. Bacteriostatic water sold separately.
> **Receipts.** Lot-specific COA at the QR on the box. Reviewed by Dr. [Name], our medical director.
> Not a substitute for clinical care. Talk to your provider.

**C. Email — first-order ship confirmation (60 words)**

> Subject: Your CaliLean order is on the way.
>
> Your order shipped this morning out of Torrance. You'll have it in 2–3 days.
>
> The COA for your lot is attached. The protocol guide is in the box.
>
> Questions about dosing or stacking? Reply to this email — a real person who reads protocols all day will answer.
>
> Run well.
> — The CaliLean team

## 5. Visual moodboard direction

**The vibe in one sentence:** *Aesop apothecary meets a Tracksmith race kit, photographed at 6am on the Manhattan Beach Strand in May light.*

### Reference brands (build moodboards against these)

- **Aesop** — restraint, apothecary typography, amber glass, clinical confidence without sterility. *Primary reference for packaging and PDP.*
- **Tracksmith** — earned-athletic, heritage-running, warm cream + navy + oxblood, editorial photography. *Primary reference for athlete photography and brand storytelling.*
- **Function Health** — clinical-direct, data-forward, confident in numbers, not afraid of medical language. *Primary reference for the education hub and clinician-of-record framing.*
- **Hodinkee** — long-form respect for craft, considered serif headlines, uncluttered white. *Reference for editorial layouts.*
- **Vuori** *(restrained side, not the lifestyle-bro side)* — California optimism without surf-cliché. *Reference for casual touchpoints (social, OOH).*
- **Maude / Glossier You-era** — minimal e-com choreography. *Reference only for storefront UX patterns, not voice.*

**Anti-references** (do not look like these):
- Bluum / Recess / Olipop pastel-grotesque
- Goop's gold-foil "luxury wellness"
- Bodybuilding.com / GymShark performance maximalism
- Generic biohacker brands (Nootropics Depot, Bulk Supplements) — utilitarian to the point of looking gray-market

### Photography direction

- **Time of day.** Pre-dawn through 9am. May Gray and June Gloom are signature, not a problem to fix. Late-afternoon golden hour as secondary.
- **Light.** Cold, salt-bleached, low-contrast. Avoid Instagram-orange grading.
- **Subjects.** Real South Bay athletes (35–55), not 22-year-old fitness models. Solo runners on the Strand. Lap pools at El Segundo. A surf check at El Porto. Hands holding a vial at a kitchen counter with a French press and a bloodwork printout.
- **Product photography.** On-white, top-down, surgical. Or: amber glass on white tile next to a single sprig of California sage. Never on a dark moody backdrop — that's gray-market peptide TikTok aesthetic.
- **Skin tones.** Real, sun-aged, age-marked. Crow's feet are an asset, not a retouch target.
- **Color grading LUT.** Cool-leaning neutral, slight green-cyan in shadows, retain skin warmth, never push contrast.

## 6. Logo direction

**Decision: wordmark-led system, not a mark-led system.**

### Rationale

The category is crowded with bad iconography (DNA helices, molecules, stylized leaves). A confident wordmark is the most defensible move — it forces all weight onto typography and craft, which is where premium peptide brands aren't competing yet. We can earn a monogram later if a real use case emerges (lapel pin, stamp, foil emboss). We do not need it on day one.

### Specs

- **Form.** Custom-drawn wordmark, all lowercase: `calilean`. Lowercase signals modern + considered (vs. all-caps, which reads "performance brand"). One word, no space, no period.
- **Posture.** Upright. No italic. No swash. No hand-drawn anything.
- **Style.** Sharp transitional or modern serif as the headline reference, but the wordmark itself should be a *low-contrast modern serif* with subtle ink-trap detailing — readable at 24px in nav, premium at 200pt on packaging. Think *GT Sectra Display* or *Domaine Display* as starting points; the final wordmark is custom, not a font drop-in.
- **Weight.** Single weight (Regular). The wordmark never has a bold variant.
- **Spacing.** Generous tracking (+20 to +40 units at display size). Confident, not cramped.
- **Lockup.** Wordmark sits alone. A horizontal rule + small clinician-of-record line ("Reviewed by [Dr. Name], MD") may sit beneath in formal contexts (packaging, footer). Never a tagline lockup on the master mark.
- **Monogram (deferred).** A `cl` ligature monogram may be developed in a v2 pass for use on:
  - Vial caps (foil-stamped or laser-etched)
  - Loyalty merch (hat, tee, water bottle)
  - Favicon / app icon
  - Clinician letterhead

### Anti-patterns

- ❌ All-caps geometric sans wordmark (reads supplement)
- ❌ Lowercase rounded grotesque à la Bluum / Recess
- ❌ Any leaf, droplet, helix, hexagon, or shield mark
- ❌ Gradient or two-tone wordmark
- ❌ "Cali" stylized differently from "Lean" (e.g., script + sans). The mark is one word.

## 7. Color and type direction

### Color

Three palettes for the designer to develop. **Lock recommendation: Palette A (Salt & Iron).**

#### Palette A — Salt & Iron *(recommended)*

Quiet, coastal, premium-clinical. Not pastel. Not warm. The brand defaults to white space and lets product earn the page.

| Role | Name | Hex | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ground | Salt | `#F4F2EC` | Warm off-white. Page background, packaging substrate. |
| Ink | Iron | `#1F2326` | Near-black with cool undertone. Body type, wordmark. |
| Accent | Pacific | `#3A5A6A` | Deep cool blue-grey. CTAs, links, COA chips. |
| Accent | Eucalyptus | `#7C8A78` | Sun-bleached green. Used sparingly — section markers, badges. |
| Signal | Coral | `#D9624A` | Warning/Rx callouts only. Never decorative. |

#### Palette B — Bone & Kelp

Warmer, more apothecary. Closer to Aesop. Risk: starts to read "spa" without strict typographic discipline.

| Role | Name | Hex |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ground | Bone | `#EDE7DA` |
| Ink | Kelp | `#22302B` |
| Accent | Driftwood | `#8C7A66` |
| Accent | Sea Glass | `#A9BBB7` |

#### Palette C — Strand at Dawn

Cooler, more editorial, more "brand of the moment." Risk: reads like a fashion brand, not a peptide brand.

| Role | Name | Hex |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ground | Fog | `#E6E8EA` |
| Ink | Tar | `#0F1213` |
| Accent | Marine | `#2A3F55` |
| Accent | Sun | `#E8C26F` |

### Color rules

- One accent color per layout. Never stack accents.
- Never use a gradient as a primary surface.
- 70% of any layout is Ground or Ink. Accents earn their place.
- Photography carries warmth — palette can stay cool and restrained.

### Type pairings

Two pairings for the designer to test. **Lock recommendation: Pairing 1 (Editorial + Precision).**

#### Pairing 1 — Editorial + Precision *(recommended)*

| Use | Family | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Display / Headline | **GT Sectra Display** (or *Domaine Display* as alt) | Sharp, transitional-modern serif. Carries the clinical-editorial tone. Used at 32px+ only. |
| Body | **Söhne** (or *Inter* as a lower-cost alt) | Precise neo-grotesque. Excellent on web at 14–18px. Sets COA tables, body, nav. |
| Mono / Technical | **Söhne Mono** (or *JetBrains Mono*) | Lot numbers, dosages, COA values, code. Trust signal. |

#### Pairing 2 — Apothecary + Modern

| Use | Family | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Display | **Tiempos Headline** | Warmer, more bookish. Pulls toward Aesop. |
| Body | **Untitled Sans** | Crisp, slightly humanist. Friendlier than Söhne. |
| Mono | **Berkeley Mono** | Editorial mono with character. |

### Type rules

- Never set body in a serif. Headlines serif, body sans, technical mono. Three-tier hierarchy, no exceptions.
- No display weights below 32px.
- Generous line-height on body (1.5–1.65).
- Numbers in body copy use tabular-figures.
- Dosages and lot numbers always in mono.
- **No Switzer.** Anywhere. (This is the cleanest tell that the rebrand is real.)

## 8. Brand voice quick test

Before any copy ships, run this five-question test:

1. Could a clinician sign their name to this without wincing?
2. Is there a number, protocol, or specific claim — or are we leaning on adjectives?
3. Would the reader feel respected or sold to?
4. Is it shorter than the first draft? (If not, cut.)
5. Does it sound like CaliLean — or does it sound like every other DTC wellness brand? (If swapping the logo wouldn't change anything, rewrite.)
