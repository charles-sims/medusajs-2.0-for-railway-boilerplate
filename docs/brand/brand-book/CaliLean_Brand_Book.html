04 · Voice Rules

Voice Rules

Do / Don't Pairs

DoDon't
BPC-157, 5mg vial, lyophilized, batch 240412-A. COA published. Premium recovery formula.
Used in published rat-model studies of tendon repair. Clinically proven to heal injuries.
For research use only. Not for human consumption. Discover the future of recovery.
Built in the South Bay. Assayed in Irvine. From the heart of California.
Short sentence. Then stop. Adjective stack. Three modifiers per noun.

Rules of Thumb

  • Sentence length: average 12 words, never over 25
  • Adjectives: zero or one per noun
  • No em-dashes, no exclamation points
  • No italicized verbs in headlines
  • No DTC trust ribbons
  • Lead with compound name and batch number
  • Cite the study or say nothing
  • RUO disclaimer on every product page

07 · Logo Usage

Logo Usage

Primary wordmark — black on white
Favicon — black
Favicon — white
Clear Space

Minimum clear space equals the cap-height of the "C" in Cali on all sides. No elements may intrude into this zone.

Minimum Size

Print: 1.5 inches wide minimum. Digital: 120px wide minimum. Below this threshold, use the favicon mark only.

Color Variants

Black on white (default). White on INK (#111111) for dark contexts. Never use PACIFIC or ALERT on the logo.

Do

  • Use on clean, uncluttered backgrounds
  • Maintain proportions when scaling
  • Use black or white variants only

Don't

  • Place on busy photography or patterns
  • Rotate, stretch, or add effects
  • Colorize the logo with brand accent colors

09 · Photography

Photography Direction

Pre-dawn May Gray on the Strand. Salt mist. Real athletes aged 35-55 from the South Bay. Shot on 35mm film with slight grain. Blue hour palette. No gym interiors. No vials on skin.

Do

  • Outdoor South Bay locations: the Strand, Dockweiler, El Porto
  • Natural light, pre-dawn or golden hour only
  • Real athletes in motion or recovery, candid
  • 35mm film grain, slightly desaturated palette
  • Blue hour tones that echo PACIFIC accent
  • Product photography on clean white or SAND surfaces
  • Negative space as a design element

Don't

  • Gym interiors, weight rooms, or treadmills
  • Vials placed on skin or held near injection sites
  • Neon lighting, saturated colors, or HDR processing
  • Stock photography or generic fitness imagery
  • Before/after transformation photos
  • Medical or clinical environments
  • Anything resembling Recess, Goop, or GymShark aesthetic
