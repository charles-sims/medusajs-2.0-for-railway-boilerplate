<Overview>
<MolecularStructure src="ss-31" />

SS-31, also known as Elamipretide or MTP-131, is a synthetic aromatic-cationic tetrapeptide with the sequence D-Arg-Dmt-Lys-Phe-NH2, where Dmt denotes 2',6'-dimethyltyrosine — a modified tyrosine residue that confers enhanced antioxidant properties and resistance to proteolytic degradation. The compound was developed by Hazel Szeto and Peter Schiller, from whose names the Szeto-Schiller (SS) peptide designation is derived. Its defining structural feature is alternating aromatic and cationic residues, which facilitate selective localization to the inner mitochondrial membrane (IMM) through electrostatic and hydrophobic interactions with cardiolipin — the signature phospholipid of that membrane.

Unlike most pharmacological agents that act at cell surface receptors or in the cytoplasm, SS-31 targets the mitochondrial inner membrane specifically, without dependence on mitochondrial membrane potential. This property — membrane-potential independence — is significant in non-clinical disease models where mitochondrial dysfunction is accompanied by collapsed membrane potential, conditions under which potential-dependent mitochondria-targeting agents lose efficacy. This targeting mechanism has made SS-31 one of the most extensively studied mitochondria-targeted peptides in preclinical research across cardiac, renal, and neurodegenerative experimental systems.

## Mechanism of Action

The central mechanism of SS-31 in experimental systems is its selective interaction with cardiolipin at the inner mitochondrial membrane. Cardiolipin is a unique dimeric phospholipid that constitutes approximately 20% of the IMM lipid composition and plays a critical structural and functional role in organizing the electron transport chain (ETC) complexes into supercomplexes — also known as respirasomes — that enhance electron transfer efficiency and reduce electron leak to molecular oxygen.<Cite id={1} /> Under conditions of oxidative stress or mitochondrial injury in non-clinical models, cardiolipin undergoes peroxidation, destabilizing ETC supercomplexes and impairing respiratory efficiency.

By binding cardiolipin, SS-31 in experimental systems has been shown to stabilize cytochrome c interactions with the IMM, preventing cytochrome c peroxidase activity that would otherwise generate additional reactive oxygen species (ROS) through cardiolipin oxidation.<Cite id={2} /> This dual function — ETC supercomplex stabilization and cytochrome c interaction normalization — positions SS-31 as a mechanistically upstream mitochondrial protective agent in non-clinical research models. In preclinical ischemia-reperfusion injury models, SS-31 treatment was associated with preserved ATP synthesis, reduced mitochondrial swelling, and attenuated apoptosis in cardiomyocytes and renal tubular cells in non-clinical experimental systems.<Cite id={3} />

<ResearchApplications>
<Application title="Cardiac Research Models">
Cardiac preclinical research represents the most extensively studied application domain for SS-31. In non-clinical models of myocardial ischemia-reperfusion injury, SS-31 administration was associated with significant reductions in infarct size, preserved left ventricular function, and maintained mitochondrial ultrastructural integrity compared to control groups. Mechanistic studies in experimental cardiac systems attributed these effects to cardiolipin protection and ETC supercomplex stabilization, which preserved oxidative phosphorylation capacity during and after experimental ischemic challenge.<Cite id={3} /><Cite id={4} /> These observations remain confined to animal models and in-vitro cardiac cell experimental systems.
</Application>

<Application title="Mitochondrial Dysfunction Studies">
SS-31 has been broadly investigated in preclinical models of primary and secondary mitochondrial dysfunction. In experimental cell systems with induced ETC complex deficiencies, SS-31 treatment was associated with partial restoration of oxygen consumption rates and ATP production indices. Non-clinical models of mitochondrial myopathy have similarly reported improved skeletal muscle function indices following SS-31 treatment, attributed to improved electron transfer efficiency within stabilized respiratory supercomplexes rather than direct enzyme replacement in experimental settings.<Cite id={1} /><Cite id={5} />
</Application>

<Application title="Aging Research">
Preclinical aging research has positioned mitochondrial dysfunction — characterized by ETC inefficiency, increased ROS production, and cardiolipin compositional changes — as a central driver of age-associated cellular decline. In non-clinical aged animal models, SS-31 administration was associated with improvements in mitochondrial respiration, reduced oxidative damage markers, and enhanced physical performance compared to age-matched controls. Experimental evidence suggests that restoring cardiolipin-dependent ETC supercomplex organization in aging mitochondria may partially reverse age-associated bioenergetic decline in non-clinical systems.<Cite id={6} />
</Application>

<Application title="Renal Research Models">
Preclinical renal research has extensively evaluated SS-31 in experimental models of acute kidney injury (AKI), cisplatin nephrotoxicity, and chronic kidney disease. In non-clinical renal ischemia-reperfusion models, SS-31 treatment was associated with preserved tubular cell viability, reduced urinary injury biomarkers, and maintained glomerular filtration indices compared to untreated controls. Mechanistic investigations in experimental renal cell systems identified mitochondrial fragmentation prevention and reduced cytochrome c release as key SS-31-associated protective events under ischemic conditions in non-clinical settings.<Cite id={7} />
</Application>

## Handling and Storage Requirements

**Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Researchers should wear nitrile gloves, laboratory coat, and safety goggles when handling SS-31 in any form. Work in a well-ventilated area or under a fume hood when reconstituting lyophilized material.

**Storage Conditions:** Store lyophilized SS-31 at -20 degrees C in a desiccated environment, protected from light. The Dmt residue within SS-31 confers enhanced chemical stability relative to native tyrosine-containing peptides; however, lyophilized storage remains essential for long-term stability. Reconstituted solutions should be stored at 2-8 degrees C and used within 48 hours, or aliquoted and stored at -20 degrees C to minimize freeze-thaw degradation.

**Spill Response:** In the event of a spill, contain the area with absorbent material, collect waste into an appropriate chemical waste container, and decontaminate the surface with 70% ethanol followed by water rinse. Dispose of all contaminated materials in accordance with institutional chemical waste protocols.

**Documentation Requirements:** Maintain detailed records of lot numbers, reconstitution dates, storage conditions, and experimental usage in compliance with institutional research protocols. All handling must conform to your institution's chemical hygiene plan and applicable regulatory requirements.
