## 0. Strategic frame

We are launching **research-use-only** peptides into a category dominated by two losers: **gray-market clinics** with no labels, and **hobby suppliers** with no design. Our wedge is the third position: **research-grade, on the record, coastal-restraint**.

The launch is not a product launch. It is a **trust launch**. Everything we do in the first 90 days has to make one point: *we publish what's in the vial.*

The South Bay is not a target market — it is the **proof market**. If the most informed, most cynical, most well-supplemented adults on the West Coast accept us, the rest of the country reads that as signal.

## 1. Narrative arc

### Day 0 — Quiet launch (private)

- **Storefront flips live at 6:00 AM Pacific** (sunrise, intentionally — first email lands while the founding list is finishing the Strand loop).
- **Founding-100 email** drops to the pre-launch waitlist. Subject line: `Read the label.`
- **No paid media on day 0.** Owned + earned only.
- **Press embargo lifts at 9:00 AM Pacific.**
- **Internal:** all-hands at 7:00 AM Pacific. CEO, CTO, CMO, Designer monitoring storefront, attestation flow, COA-link integrity.

### Days 1-30 — Founding 100 + earned media

The first 30 days optimize for **the photo of the lab book on the founding customer's coffee table that ends up on Instagram unprompted**.

Mile markers:
- **Days 1-7:** founding-100 waitlist redeems. Fulfillment + lab-book mailings within 48h.
- **Days 7-14:** first earned-media wave (LA-local + Tier 1 health vertical).
- **Days 14-21:** **The Strand pop-up** — Sunday morning, free coffee, COAs printed on a folding table. No sales.
- **Days 21-30:** post-pop-up content drop.

**KPIs (30 days):**
- 100/100 founding spots redeemed.
- 3+ placed pieces of earned media.
- 500+ net new emails on waitlist.
- 40%+ of founding 100 share unprompted on social.
- Zero attestation-flow defects in production.

### Days 30-60 — Open the door, turn on the engine

- **Day 31:** general access opens. Referral engine goes live.
- **Days 30-45:** referral program (1 referral = 1 free 5mg vial for the friend).
- **Days 45-60:** first creator seeding. Education hub launches with long-form essays per research pathway.

**KPIs (60 days):**
- 1,000 paying customers, 35%+ from referral.
- 5 placed creator features.
- Education hub essays live and indexed.

### Days 60-90 — Earn the expansion + national signal

- **Day 61:** Phase 2 SKU expansion if warranted by demand and COA readiness.
- **Days 60-75:** first OOH placement — single PCH billboard.
- **Days 75-90:** national tier press push.
- **Day 90:** lab book v2 ships to founding 100 unannounced.

**KPIs (90 days):**
- 3,000 paying customers, 40%+ repeat rate.
- Two national-tier outlets placed.
- Storefront NPS 60+ at order #2.
- Inventory turns 2x+ on lead SKUs.

## 2. Hero campaign — "Read the Label."

### 2.1 Big idea

**Read the Label.** The peptide market hides what's inside the vial. We publish the lab assay. Reading the label is the most subversive thing a customer can do in this category, and we are the only brand asking them to do it.

### 2.2 Headline + master copy

**Headline (locked):**
> Read the Label.

**Subhead (locked):**
> Peptides, on the record. Recovery, leanness, longevity. Research-grade, independently tested, built in the South Bay.

**Master body (50 words):**
> Most peptides in this country are sold without a label. We publish the certificate of analysis on every batch, the assay numbers in plain English, and the lot you received in the box. Read it. If you can't read theirs, walk away.

### 2.3 Three supporting beats

#### Beat 1 — "What's in the Vial." (Days 0-14)

The COA-as-art beat. Mass spectrometry traces, chromatograms, batch numbers — printed at scale. The artifact *is* the ad.

- **Visual:** a real CaliLean batch chromatogram on `--cl-bg`. Type only. No vial.
- **Channel cuts:** PDP hero, founding email v1, day-0 social carousel.

#### Beat 2 — "Built for the Strand." (Days 14-60)

The geo/audience beat. Real South Bay athletes, pre-dawn light.

- **Visual:** 35mm-style pre-dawn Strand photography. Vial in still-life, never on skin.
- **Channel cuts:** OOH, paid social (geo-fenced SB + Westside), Strand pop-up.

#### Beat 3 — "Studied. Not Promised." (Days 45-90)

The restraint/RUO beat. Cite the paper. Show the study. Refuse the outcome claim.

- **Visual:** a published study cover flat on `--cl-sand`, highlighted in `--cl-pacific`.
- **Channel cuts:** education hub, long-form social, press pitch art, lab book v2.

### 2.4 Production guardrails

- No exclamation points anywhere.
- No em-dashes in marketing copy. Use periods.
- Average sentence length 14 words max. Body copy never over 25.
- Photography: 35mm-style, pre-dawn or May Gray, real South Bay athletes 30-55. Vials in still-life, never on skin.
- **Type: Plus Jakarta Sans 700 for headlines. Plus Jakarta Sans 400-600 for body. JetBrains Mono for batch/dose/data.**
- The Bluum Switzer-pastel direction is dead.
- **CL- naming on all metabolic product creative.** Never spell out the generic drug name in campaign materials.

## 4. Founding-customer playbook (first 100)

### 4.1 Acquisition (T-30 to T-0)

Built off-internet: IRL seeding at South Bay venues, run-club partnerships, founder dinners. Goal: ~2,000 waitlist signups, 60%+ South Bay zips.

### 4.2 The Founding 100 offer

- Numbered slots, 1/100 to 100/100. Each ships a numbered lab book (32-page perfect-bound).
- Standard pricing. **No discount.** The lab book is the gift.
- Slots that don't redeem in 14 days cycle to waitlist.
- $1/order to Surfrider Foundation South Bay. Symbolic disclosure.

### 4.3 Fulfillment as marketing

- Matte recycled box, single-color wordmark, vial in molded foam.
- COA QR on the inner box flap (the customer opens it to find the trust).
- Lab book ships separately in kraft mailer within 7 days. Two-touch arrival.

### 4.4 Referral (Day 31, not Day 1)

- 1 referral = free 5mg vial for the new customer.
- Referrer at 5 referrals: hand-signed thank-you card.
- Referrer at 10 referrals: lab book v2.

## 6. What good looks like at Day 90

- A founding customer whose lab book sits on their coffee table, who refers two friends without being asked.
- A national-tier reporter writing *"the brand that publishes the certificate of analysis"* without us suggesting it.
- A South Bay run club that calls itself a CaliLean run club without us paying them to.
- An education hub with essays ranking for queries we did not pay for.
- Zero instances of generic drug names in any customer-facing copy across all 15 SKUs.

What does not count: discount-driven volume, paid-creator endorsements, any version of "10x recovery" copy.
