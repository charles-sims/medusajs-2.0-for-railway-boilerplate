## 0. Strategic frame

We are launching **research-use-only** peptides into a category dominated by two losers: **gray-market clinics** with no labels, and **hobby suppliers** with no design. Our wedge is the third position: **research-grade, on the record, coastal-restraint**.

The launch is not a product launch. It is a **trust launch**. Everything we do in the first 90 days has to make one point: *we publish what's in the vial.*

The South Bay is not a target market — it is the **proof market**. If the most informed, most cynical, most well-supplemented adults on the West Coast accept us, the rest of the country reads that as signal. National comes after. Local first, locally proven, then everywhere else.

## 1. Narrative arc

### Day 0 — Quiet launch (private)

- **Storefront flips live at 6:00 AM Pacific** (sunrise, intentionally — first email lands while the founding list is finishing the Strand loop).
- **Founding-100 email** drops to the pre-launch waitlist (see §4). Subject line: `Read the label.` No body copy beyond that, the master tagline, a vial photo, and a numbered claim link.
- **No paid media on day 0.** Owned + earned only. Paid spends light a fire only after the fire is already lit.
- **Press embargo lifts at 9:00 AM Pacific.** GQ Health vertical, L.A. Magazine, and Manhattan Beach Confidential have pre-briefed pieces ready to go (see §3).
- **Internal:** all-hands at 7:00 AM Pacific. CEO, CTO, CMO, Designer on a single thread monitoring for storefront issues, attestation flow, COA-link integrity. Anything that breaks in the first 4 hours gets fixed in the first 4 hours.

### Days 1–30 — Founding 100 + earned media

The first 30 days are about **redeeming the founding spots and shipping the lab books** (see §4). We do not optimize for volume. We optimize for **the photo of the lab book on the founding customer's coffee table that ends up on Instagram unprompted**.

Mile markers:

- **Days 1–7:** founding-100 waitlist redeems. Fulfillment + lab-book mailings go out within 48h of order.
- **Days 7–14:** first earned-media wave lands (LA-local + Tier 1 health vertical if landed). CMO + CEO available for a single 30-min phoner per outlet, no more.
- **Days 14–21:** **The Strand pop-up** (see §2.4) — Sunday morning, free coffee, COAs printed and laid out on a folding table. No sales at the pop-up. Just the table, the vials, and the people who made them.
- **Days 21–30:** post-pop-up content drop. Photos run as the second creative wave on owned channels.

**KPIs (30 days):**
- 100/100 founding spots redeemed.
- ≥3 placed pieces of earned media (1 national, 2 LA-local).
- ≥500 net new emails on the waitlist (post-launch waitlist for the next opening).
- ≥40% of founding 100 share unprompted on social.
- Zero attestation-flow defects in production.

### Days 30–60 — Open the door, turn on the engine

We **open beyond the founding 100** on day 31. Referral engine goes live. First creator seeding lands.

Mile markers:

- **Day 31:** general access opens. Cap quantities per SKU per customer (no resellers). Inventory throttled to keep stockouts visible without faking scarcity.
- **Days 30–45:** referral program (`1 referral → 1 free 5mg vial for the friend, the referrer gets the lab book`). Referral comes from the founding 100's actual lives — Crossfit boxes, run clubs, surf clubs, F45 and Equinox South Bay locker-room conversations.
- **Days 45–60:** first creator seeding ships. Local first (Manhattan Beach run club leads, South Bay triathlete community accounts), national-aspirational seeds at the same time but with zero expectations (Huberman, Attia, Patrick, Bryan Johnson — see §3.2).
- **Days 45–60:** education hub launches with three long-form essays (one per pillar peptide) — written by the medical advisor, edited to brand voice by CMO. Each essay cites primary literature, no therapeutic claims, links to current COA.

**KPIs (60 days):**
- 1,000 paying customers, 35%+ from referral codes.
- 5 placed creator features (paid or organic — local OK).
- 3 education-hub essays live, indexed, ranking on at least one informational query each.
- Average sentence length on storefront ≤ 14 words (auto-checked in CI; CTO instrumented).

### Days 60–90 — Earn the second SKU wave + the national signal

Days 60–90 is **the test that we are a brand and not a campaign**. We add Tier-2 SKUs only after the lead 5 are clean on COA, on returns, and on customer feedback. We push the first national-tier press (Bloomberg Pursuits, Outside, GQ feature if not done in window 1). We take our first paid-media swing — narrowly geo-fenced South Bay + adjacent (PV, Westside).

Mile markers:

- **Day 61:** Tier-2 SKUs go live (see §6, M5/SKA-12 will define the line; this narrative assumes a 5+5 split — 5 launch, 5 second-wave).
- **Days 60–75:** first **OOH placement** — single PCH billboard, El Segundo or Manhattan Beach Boulevard. One vial. Batch number readable. Tagline. That's it. (See §2.4.)
- **Days 75–90:** national tier press. Bloomberg Pursuits longevity vertical, Outside coastal athlete vertical, GQ Recovery feature.
- **Day 90:** **the lab book v2 ships to the founding 100 — unannounced.** Prints the second batch of COAs, the next three essays, and a thank-you. This is the move that makes them lifers.

**KPIs (90 days):**
- 3,000 paying customers, ≥40% repeat rate.
- Two of the four national-tier outlets placed (GQ, Outside, Bloomberg, WSJ Off Duty).
- ≥1 unprompted, organic Huberman/Attia/Patrick mention in their content (gift-only seeding — we do not pay for endorsement).
- Storefront NPS ≥ 60 measured at order #2.
- Inventory turns ≥ 2x for the lead 5 SKUs.

## 2. Hero campaign — "Read the Label."

### 2.1 Big idea

**Read the Label.** Two words. It is a dare and an instruction. The peptide market hides what's inside the vial — gray-market clinics will not even tell you the lot number. We publish the lab assay. Reading the label is the most subversive thing a customer can do in this category, and we are the only brand asking them to do it.

The campaign is the **anti-gray-market** campaign. Every beat reinforces transparency over hype.

### 2.2 Headline + master copy

**Headline (locked):**
> Read the Label.

**Subhead (locked):**
> Peptides, on the record. Recovery, leanness, longevity. Research-grade, independently tested, built in the South Bay.

**Master body (50 words, used in OOH cuts and the founding email):**
> Most peptides in this country are sold without a label. We publish the certificate of analysis on every batch, the assay numbers in plain English, and the lot you received in the box. Read it. If you can't read theirs, walk away.

### 2.3 Three supporting beats

Each beat is a complete sub-campaign — its own creative, its own headline, its own channel mix. They run in sequence over the 90 days. Beat 1 is the day-0 wave. Beats 2 and 3 layer in.

#### Beat 1 — "What's in the Vial."

**The COA-as-art beat.** Treat the certificate of analysis like editorial photography. Mass spectrometry traces, chromatograms, batch numbers — printed at scale. The artifact *is* the ad.

- **Headline:** *What's in the Vial.*
- **Visual:** a real CaliLean batch chromatogram printed on `--cl-bg`. Type only. No vial.
- **Channel cuts:** PDP hero swap (replace placeholder hero), founding email v1, day-0 social (a single carousel: vial → label → COA → batch number).
- **Window:** Days 0–14.

#### Beat 2 — "Built for the Strand."

**The geo/audience beat.** The South Bay is the proof market. Real local athletes, pre-dawn light, well-worn kit. The vial sits on concrete, tile, or a clipboard — never on skin (medicalizes too quickly).

- **Headline:** *Built for the Strand.*
- **Visual:** 35mm-style pre-dawn Strand photography per the brand brief §5. Real South Bay athletes, 30–55, mid-stride or mid-stretch. The vial appears as a still-life cut, not a held object.
- **Channel cuts:** OOH (PCH billboard, see §2.4), paid social geo-fenced South Bay + Westside, Strand pop-up POS, founding email v2.
- **Window:** Days 14–60.

#### Beat 3 — "Studied. Not Promised."

**The restraint/RUO beat.** Cite the paper. Show the rat-model study. State the form factor. Refuse the outcome claim. This is the beat that turns the legal posture into the brand posture.

- **Headline:** *Studied. Not Promised.*
- **Visual:** a published study cover (rights cleared) photographed flat on `--cl-sand`, with one passage highlighted in `--cl-pacific`. The product is implicit.
- **Channel cuts:** education hub launch creative, long-form social (carousel essays), Bloomberg/Outside/GQ pitch art, day-90 lab book v2 cover.
- **Window:** Days 45–90.

### 2.4 Channel cuts (master grid)

| Channel | Beat 1 (0–14) | Beat 2 (14–60) | Beat 3 (45–90) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Storefront hero | COA-as-art | Strand still-life | Study + restraint copy |
| Email (owned) | Founding-100 launch | Referral activation | Lab book v2 announce |
| Paid social (geo: SB + Westside) | Off | Light spend, Beat 2 cuts | Layered Beat 2 + 3 |
| OOH | None | One PCH billboard, El Segundo or MB Blvd | Hold |
| Direct mail | Lab book v1 to founding 100 | None | Lab book v2 to founding 100 |
| Pop-up | None | Strand pop-up week 3 | None |
| PR | LA-local + Tier 1 health | Local creator features | National-tier features |
| Education hub | Pre-tease | Launch (3 essays) | Expand (3 more essays) |

**Production guardrails (carry from brand brief §5):**
- No exclamation points anywhere.
- No em-dashes in marketing copy. Use periods.
- Average sentence length ≤ 14 words. Body copy never over 25.
- Photography: 35mm-style, pre-dawn or May Gray, real South Bay athletes 30–55, never Lululemon-new kit. Vials in still-life, never on skin.
- Type: GT Sectra Display for headlines. Söhne for body. Söhne Mono for batch/dose/data.
- The Bluum Switzer-pastel direction is dead. Anything that drifts back toward it gets killed in review.

## 4. Founding-customer playbook (first 100 customers — South Bay-led)

The first 100 are not customers. They are **the brand's first artifact**. Every decision below is built around making them want to show the box.

### 4.1 Acquisition — pre-launch (T-30 to T-0)

The waitlist is built **off-internet**, deliberately. Online ads pre-launch would dilute the signal.

- **IRL seeding** at 12 South Bay venues: Equinox (MB + ES), F45 (Hermosa), OrangeTheory (Redondo), 3 Crossfit boxes, 2 surf shops (ET Surf + Spyder), Becker Surfboards, Manhattan Beach Cycle, recovery studios (Restore Hyper Wellness ES, Pause Studio).
  - Drop a printed card. Wordmark, tagline, QR to a single-line waitlist page. No body copy.
- **Run-club + tri-club partnerships:** Manhattan Beach Run Club, Hermosa Beach Run Club, South Bay Triathlon Club. Three pre-launch pop-ups at their Saturday meetups — coffee, no pitch, the cards.
- **Founder dinners:** CEO + CMO host two 8-person dinners at a Manhattan Beach restaurant. Invite-only, hand-picked from the IRL drop list. No product. Conversation.

**Goal:** ~2,000 waitlist signups, ≥60% with South Bay zips (90245, 90254, 90266, 90274, 90277, 90278).

### 4.2 The Founding 100 offer

- **Numbered slots, 1/100 → 100/100.** Each slot ships a **numbered lab book** (32-page perfect-bound booklet, printed locally — not POD). The book contains: brand letter, the 5 launch peptides with primary-literature citations, every batch's COA from launch through day 90 (mailed as a v2 supplement at day 90), and a single black-ink illustration per peptide.
- **Pricing:** standard SKU pricing. **No discount.** The lab book is the gift, not a percentage off. Discounting at launch poisons the brand.
- **Founding code:** redemption requires a code in the founding email. Cannot be referred, cannot be transferred. Slots that don't redeem in 14 days cycle to the waitlist.
- **Local nonprofit hook:** $1 per founding-100 order goes to the **Surfrider Foundation South Bay chapter**. Disclosed on the order confirmation. Total $100 — symbolic, not the marketing budget. The point is the disclosure, not the dollar.

### 4.3 Fulfillment as marketing

- **Packaging:** matte recycled box, single-color wordmark, no glossy stickers. Vial in molded foam with the printed batch tag. The COA QR is on the inner box flap, not the outer — the customer has to open it to find the receipt of trust.
- **Insert:** one card. Two sentences from the CEO. Hand-numbered (1/100 etc.) by the team in the first 100 boxes.
- **Lab book ships separately within 7 days** of redemption — by USPS, in a kraft mailer. The two-touch arrival creates the second moment.

### 4.4 Referral — turned on Day 31, not Day 1

We hold the referral engine until day 31 because:
- Letting founding-100 customers refer in week 1 dilutes the "founding" signal.
- Holding it makes the slot artifically scarce in a way the customer respects (because we explained why).

Mechanic:
- 1 referral = a free 5mg vial for the new customer.
- Referrer gets nothing transactional. Referrer gets **a hand-signed thank-you card** at 5 referrals.
- Referrer at 10 referrals gets the lab book v2 (the day-90 supplement).

### 4.5 The math

To reach 100 paying founding customers we need ~2,000 waitlist sign-ups (5% conversion from waitlist to paid is the floor; transparency-led brands typically see 8–12% on a controlled launch). That means we need ~150 cards in market across 12 venues with ~8 cards per QR pickup. We will ship 2,000 cards. Cost: ~$400 print + ~$1,500 venue partnerships + ~$3,000 founder dinners. **Total founding-customer acquisition spend: ≤ $5,000.**

Lab books: 100 units × ~$18 print/bind = $1,800. **Total founding-100 program cost: ≤ $7,000, ex product COGS.**

## 7. What good looks like at Day 90

- A founding customer whose lab book sits on their coffee table, who refers two friends without being asked, who reads the COA when the new batch arrives.
- A national-tier reporter writing the sentence *"the brand that publishes the certificate of analysis"* without us suggesting it.
- A South Bay run club that calls itself a CaliLean run club without us paying them to.
- An education hub with three essays that rank for queries we did not pay for.
- A second SKU wave that ships because the first wave's COAs and customer feedback earned it.

What does not count: discount-driven volume, paid-creator endorsements, any version of "10x recovery" copy.
