<Overview>
Bacteriostatic Water (BAC Water) is sterile water for injection containing 0.9% benzyl alcohol (9 mg/mL) as a bacteriostatic preservative. The benzyl alcohol inhibits the growth of most common microorganisms, distinguishing BAC Water from single-use sterile water by permitting multiple punctures of the vial septum over an extended period. In laboratory research settings, BAC Water is the standard solvent used for reconstituting lyophilized peptides and other research compounds intended for use in non-clinical in-vitro or preclinical animal model experiments.

Once opened, BAC Water vials can be used for multiple withdrawals for up to 28 days when stored under appropriate conditions. Each withdrawal must be performed using aseptic technique to maintain the bacteriostatic integrity of the solution. BAC Water is compatible with the majority of research peptides at physiologically relevant pH ranges; however, compatibility with specific compounds should be verified prior to use in experimental protocols.

CaliLean offers BAC Water as a research ancillary product, intended strictly for use in reconstituting research compounds for in-vitro and preclinical experimental applications.
</Overview>

<Safety>
## Handling and Storage Requirements

Store at controlled room temperature (15–30 degrees C). Do not freeze. Discard any unused portion after 28 days from first use. Prior to each withdrawal, disinfect the vial septum with 70% isopropyl alcohol and allow to dry completely. Use aseptic technique throughout all reconstitution procedures. Inspect the solution before each use; discard if particulates, discoloration, or cloudiness are observed.

**This product is for Research Use Only (RUO). Not for human or veterinary diagnostic or therapeutic use.**
